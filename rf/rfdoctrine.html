    • Why doctrines?

    1. Novel technological advantages erode over time.
    2. Timing & Training alone cannot sustain technology movements over longer term.
    3. Cultivate a culture of sustainability for technology accomplishments.
    4. Cultivate a culture of change for continued improvement of technology accomplishments.
  • Help us to continue to stay relevant and true to the technological accomplishments.
  • Allows us to mix in various thoughs of programming and nature of programmers.
  • Allows us to cultivate a culture of change.
  • Establish and maintain a process of change valued under a common set of principles.
  • Disagreements, dialects and diversity of thought are needed for sustainability.

    • Eight pillars

    1. Culture of simplicity & sustainability (SaS).
    2. Optimize for developer happiness.
    3. Convention over configuration (CoC).
    4. Ideological flexibility over purity (FoP).
    5. Progress over stability (PoS).
    6. Value focus over technology focus (VoT).
    7. Facts over feelings (FoF).
    8. Idealize over Idolize (IoI).
  • Simplicity & sustainability
    1. Will guide us through the need of being nible, simple and understandable.
    2. Allows us to mature the process through simple steps.
  • Optimize for developer happiness.
    1. Look through the eyes of developer.
  • Convention Over configuration
    1. Discuss the conventions lead to a common understanding
    2. Shared understanding and implicit contracts for dependencies.
  • Ideological flexibility over purity
    1. Think from the prespective of solution rather than tools.
    2. Pick the right tool for the job.
    3. Bring in the new ideas from other areas to create a cohesive solution.
    4. Push us to think from different prespectives.
  • Progress over stability
    1. Natural tendency for ossification.
    2. Past behaviors will be an issue for someone.
    3. Progress is measured by our ability and willingsness to push the change.
  • Value focus over technology focus.
    1. Glorious majestic monolith over simple distributed systems.
    2. Use technology to create value oriented simple systems.
    3. Polygot technology elements based on value.
  • Facts over feelings
    1. Identify facts vs opinions.
    2. Access opinions based on value creation ability.
    3. Mesaure advancements (value & technology) in quantitative terms.
  • Idealize over Idolize
    1. Idealize - Regard or represent as perfect or better than in reality.
    2. Idolize - Admire, revere, or love greatly or excessively.
    3. Ideals - help us grow; Idols - hinder growth & encourage subordination.
    4. Ideals - improve quality; Idols - improve ego.

    1. Scalable design - excellent theory, difficult to practice.
    2. Release & Update often.
    3. Code towards logical & cyclomatic simplicity
    4. Code has cost. Justify it.

    Culture of simplicity & sustainability (SoS) - Cont.

  • Characteristics of Unsustainable environment
    1. Primarily Reactive.
    2. Code first, fix defects later.
    3. Death Spiral
  • Characteristics of Sustainable environment
    1. Proactive - high quality, reliability & continous improvement.
    2. Value Creation - Continous improvement & Efficiency
    3. Virtuous Cycle
      1. Higher Team morale
      2. Ability to deal with Increasing complexity & change.
      3. Balance in needs - Long & Short term.

    • Convention over configuration (CoC).

    1. Avoid unworthy recurrent deliberations & make progress in areas that really matter.
    2. Good conventions pay dividents across wide spectrum.
      1. Lowers the barriers of entry.
      2. Each part is same or very similar to other areas that went before.
      3. A place for everything & everything in its place.
    3. Constraints liberate the most able minds.

    Ideological flexibility over purity (FoP).

    1. Composite of many Ideological choices and paradigms.
    2. Not a championship where superior idea is declared a winner.
    3. Together - Stronger and more capable.
    4. Deversity in people, paradigms & thoughts.
    5. Accept complex journey of learning as a joy, not an obstacle.
  • Encourage anyone in the minority group to speak up early to give them a chance to influence the direction of the conversation before a cascade sets in.
  • When one person goes against the majority, the likelyhood that others will offer divergent prespectives increases greatly. Take advantage of this phenomenon to promote healthy dissent.

    • Progress over stability (PoS).

    1. Systems has natural tendency for ossification.
    2. Evolution will help survival and prosperity in the future.
    3. Progress is about people and willingsness to push change.
      1. There are no lifetime seats.
      2. Acquire strength to debug the busted, figure it out, and move with the times.
    4. Welcome and encourage new ideas (not ossified old ideas). Need fresh blood and fresh ideas to make better progress.

    Value focus over technology focus (VoT).

    1. Value minded choices over technology.
    2. Sustainable value. Grow with the change.
    3. Glorious Majestic Monolith over Simple distributed systems.
    4. Polygot technology choices based on value creation, simplicity & sustainability.

    Facts over feelings (FoF)

    1. Identify facts vs opinions.
    2. Deliberate opinions based on value creation & sustainability.
    3. Quantitative measurements vs Qualitative.
    4. Quantify
      1. Ability to change
      2. Sustainability - growth & technology
      3. Value creation.
      5. “Too often we . . . enjoy the comfort of opinion without the discomfort of thought.”
      ~ John F Kennedy

    Idealize over Idolize (IoI)

    1. Idealize - thoughts, ideas & paradigms.
    2. Never idolize - thoughts, opinions ...
    3. Ready to change the ideals.
    4. Deliberate and look for counter points.
    5. Welcome diversity
      6. “Everyone should be respected as an individual, but no one idolized.”
      ~ Albert Einstein
